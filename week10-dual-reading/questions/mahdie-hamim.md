# Week 10 – HYBHY Chapter 7 + Producing Open Source Software Chapter 4

**Author:** Hamim Mahdie

## Question

Chapter 7 discusses how going over your manager’s head in a corporate hierarchy is risky because authority flows through a formal chain of command, and even well-intentioned actions can damage trust. From my own experience in internships (both in tech and non-tech roles), I’ve seen situations where managers were either highly competent or clearly misaligned with project needs, which made this tradeoff very real. In a particular scenario, I asked my manager to clarify on some specifics of a project we were building and instead of properly consulting the project specifics, he ended up misguiding and derailing my current and future work that costed us a whole week of development.

At the same time, Chapter 4 of Producing Open Source Software describes a system where authority comes from contribution and reputation, and where decisions are often made through open discussion rather than strict hierarchy.

Given these differences, when someone in charge is ineffective or blocking progress, how is escalating the issue different in these two systems like going over your manager’s head in a company versus speaking up publicly or forking a project in open source? Does open source actually make it easier to deal with bad leadership, or do similar power constraints still exist in practice?
